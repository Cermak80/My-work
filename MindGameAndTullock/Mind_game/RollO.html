Part 1

In the experiment, you will be matched with another participant, whom we will call your opponent.

Your opponent was asked to think of any two numbers from 1 to 6 and remember them. He/she was then asked to roll a die and report whether the number he/she rolled was one of the two numbers he/she thought of. There are two possible outcomes of the game:

  • Your opponent reports that the rolled number is one of the two numbers he/she thought of. Then your opponent will receive a payment of £2, and you will be paid £0.
  • Your opponent reports that the rolled number is not one of the two numbers he/she thought of. Then your opponent will receive a payment of £0, and you will be paid £2.
